Making NHS IT less bad

By building the foundations to understand the way that health care is delivered, and showing how we can build upon those foundations, we help the people shaping the delivery of medicine to make great, informed decisions.

By building applications that help front-line medical staff to work smarter, we smooth the rough edges of patient care, letting people spend more time healing the sick and less time grappling with the system.

Openness

We believe that the peer review and accountability that medical research benefits from should also be used to help the tools that we use. Which is why we use an open source stack for our building blocks, and publish our code.

Groucho Marx CHAIRMAN OF THE BOARD, CEO AND PRESIDENT

Julius Henry "Groucho" Marx (October 2, 1890 – August 19, 1977) was an American comedian and film and television star. He is known as a master of quick wit and widely considered one of the best comedians of the modern era. His rapid-fire, often impromptu delivery of innuendo-laden patter earned him many admirers and imitators

Chico Marx VICE CHAIRMAN OF THE BOARD

Leonard "Chico" Marx (March 22, 1887 – October 11, 1961) was an American comedian and film star as part of the Marx Brothers. His persona in the act was that of a dim-witted albeit crafty con artist, seemingly of rural Italian origin, who wore shabby clothes, and sported a curly-haired wig and Tyrolean hat.

Harpo Marx CHAIRMAN OF VICE

Adolph "Harpo" Marx (later Arthur "Harpo" Marx) (November 23, 1888 – September 28, 1964) was an American comedian and film star. He was the second-oldest of the Marx Brothers. His comic style was influenced by clown and pantomime traditions. He wore a curly reddish blonde wig, and never spoke during performances (he blew a horn or whistled to communicate). Marx frequently used props such as a horn cane, made up of a lead pipe, tape, and a bulbhorn, and he played the harp in most of his films.
